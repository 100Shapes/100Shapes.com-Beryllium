Project Freebird was designed to present external content on the BBC website while staying true to the broadcaster's core principle of balance.

The decision to feature external msuci reviews and articles on its own branded platformwas a major departure for the BBC. Our brief was to take the BBC's sophisticated new content-browsing algorithm and apply it to create meaningful music discovery experiences.

We designed, built and audience-tested a Freebird module with its own visual identity that summarises and presents a clear balance of music news and criticism. Feedback on the recent pilot has been highly positive from audiences and stakeholders, and shown that the BBC can offer a quality curatorial service while safeguarding its own brand. The BBC is considering ways Freebird can be applied across its wider digital innovation strategy.
