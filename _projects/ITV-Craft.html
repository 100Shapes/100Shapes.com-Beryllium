ITV’s Global Entertainment Operations ('GE Ops') team distribute content around the globe, overseeing the transfer of files and data to other broadcasters worldwide. For years they had been hamstrung by legacy tools and convoluted processes that were slow, inefficient and error-prone.

We sat with the team and stepped through their day-to-day processes. To make a difference as quickly as possible, we identified one of the worst frustration points and set about rapidly developing and testing a new user interface design.

This became Craft, a ‘translator layer’ that sits over the legacy database and immediately reduced one frequent order-fulfilment task from eight minutes to 20 seconds. We’re continuing to add functions to Craft, extending its usefulness to other tasks and other departments in the organisation.

This has been a hugely satisfying project for the 100 Shapes team – a brilliant example of how UX design can make people’s daily work lives tangibly better.
